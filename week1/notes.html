From the video "Master Mobile UX"

  • mobile first
    • design for the smallest. makes you focus on the content
    • makes a small simple device
  • responsive does not necessarily mean mobile first
  • mobile first means you are ready for the future
  • desktop purchasing is down
  • mobile is not a fad. A lot more will come out
  • Steve Jobs: people don't know what they want until you show it to them
  • don't make users hunt
  • be quick!
  • most people use one hand for a small device. Think about reach
  • no attention span these days!
    • slow site = decrease in usage, revenue, etc.
  • speed matters
    • reduce image usage
    • webfonts are lower bandwidth
  • think about moving menus to the bottom to be more reachable
  • create perception of speed
  • need to do research and figure out what users do and therefore need
  • make it simple!
    • reduce cognitive load
    • make checkboxes larger
    • break forms into sections - but show progress
    • auto-complete for forms
    • passwords are frustrating. Show password to user
    • for number entry - just give the number pad
    • link phone numbers instead of making the user have to remember or copy/paste
  • end users shape the market
  • pop-ups are the most detested
  • make ads nice and simple
  • users don't come to a site for ads
  • with ads, empathy is huge - think of what you would want
